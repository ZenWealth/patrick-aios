## Company Summary

GOIA Technologies Ltd is an independent cyber governance advisory firm incorporated in England and Wales in February 2025 (Company No. 16228859). It provides structured governance assessments to FCA-regulated IFA and wealth management firms, delivering board-level visibility of their cyber risk position against the NCSC Cyber Assessment Framework and FCA operational resilience expectations.

GOIA operates above the IT layer. It does not overlap with IT managed services, technical implementation, or regulated financial advice. Its purpose is to close the governance gap between what directors are accountable for under FCA rules and what their current documentation can demonstrate.

GOIA is not FCA-authorised. It is ICO-registered (ZB961281). It holds Cyber Essentials certification and professional indemnity insurance (£2M PI, £1M public liability).

## Methodology

Every assessment is structured against four frameworks simultaneously: NCSC Cyber Assessment Framework (CAF) — 8 domains; FCA PS21/3 Operational Resilience; NIST CSF v2.0; CIS Controls v8; with Consumer Duty and UK GDPR applied across relevant domains.

All findings connect to at least one of three business consequences: Revenue Continuity, Regulatory Exposure, or Client Trust and Firm Value.

**GOIA BoardSignal** is the proprietary methodology through which technical scan outputs (Guardz) are translated into board-level governance narratives. It is GOIA intellectual property developed by Gerard Ouattara. The execution architecture uses n8n (orchestration), Mistral AI (extraction), Perplexity (enrichment), and PDFShift (rendering). Gerard reviews, edits, and approves every BoardSignal output before it reaches any client. BoardSignal is in active Phase 1 deployment.

## Target Market

FCA-regulated IFA firms, wealth management firms, networks, and compliance support providers in the UK. Typical client profile: advisory firm without an internal CISO, reliant on an external IT provider, with board-level accountability under FCA PS21/3 and Consumer Duty.
