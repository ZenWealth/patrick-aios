## Current Focus Period

Q2–Q3 2026 — Transition from proposition development to active commercialisation.

## Strategic Priorities

### 1. Secure Strategic Distribution

The highest-priority objective. GOIA will not scale through direct sales — the model depends on distribution partners who already serve regulated advice firms. Target partner types:

- Compliance consultancies (e.g., ComplyPort — currently in due diligence)
- Regulatory support and outsourced compliance providers
- Networks and service providers to advice firms
- Professional membership organisations
- Wealth management platforms and service firms

The ComplyPort relationship is the most advanced. GOIA submitted a full methodology and working papers pack (v1.2, May 2026) to ComplyPort's RegTech and FinTech Directors Group for evaluation. Discussions have been encouraging. No formal agreement signed as of June 2026.

### 2. Convert Thought Leadership into Commercial Engagement

GOIA and Patrick have built significant authority through:
- Articles in IFA Magazine
- Industry presentations and webinars
- The Ask Patrick Cyber Clinic AI platform
- Operational resilience thought leadership content

The next phase is converting that authority into qualified conversations, pilot engagements, and first paying clients.

### 3. Finalise Legal and Operational Infrastructure

- Engagement letter liability cap clause: awaiting sign-off from UK commercial solicitor (open action, June 2026)
- API key rotation protocol documented post-EMB Global handover (1 May 2026)
- BoardSignal Phase 1 improvement roadmap to follow first live client cohort

## What Success Looks Like (Next 3–6 Months)

- At least one strategic distribution agreement signed
- First cohort of paying clients delivered through that channel
- ComplyPort relationship formalised (or alternative distribution path identified)
- Engagement letter template cleared for live use

GOIA becomes the recognised cyber governance and operational resilience specialist for the UK financial advice profession, operating primarily through trusted intermediaries and strategic partnerships. Revenue model anchored in the retainer (Stage 4), with Snapshot and Executive Review as the acquisition pathway.
