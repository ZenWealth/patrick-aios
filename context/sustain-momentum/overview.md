# Sustain Momentum Ltd — Overview

> Patrick's consulting, thought leadership, and specialist advisory business.

---

## Company Summary

Sustain Momentum Ltd is Patrick Murphy's personal consulting, coaching, and thought leadership business, founded February 2025. It operates across several connected domains where Patrick has deep practitioner expertise: financial planning practice, behavioural finance, AI in financial services, and regulatory change — particularly the FCA's Advice Guidance Boundary Review (AGBR) and Targeted Support (TS).

## What Sustain Momentum Does

Sustain Momentum operates across two distinct audiences: **consumers** (individuals seeking financial clarity) and **firms** (advice businesses, platforms, and networks navigating regulatory change).

### Consumer-Facing: Financial Clarity and Coaching

Website: sustain-momentum.com

Sustain Momentum is positioned around closing the *intention-to-action gap* — the disconnect between knowing what to do financially and actually doing it. Core tagline: *"Clear thinking for better decisions, stronger finances, and a life that feels more aligned."*

Services include:
- **Clarity Check** — diagnostic to identify what matters and what's blocking progress
- **Personal Clarity Review** — one-to-one guidance sessions
- **Clarity Call** — dedicated consultation

Explicitly not FCA-regulated financial advice. No product sales. Behavioural and educational in nature. Rooted in Patrick's belief that people's real obstacle is emotional and behavioural, not informational.

### B2B: Targeted Support & AGBR Specialist Consulting

Website: targeted.support (dedicated platform — see below)

Patrick advises consultancy firms, platforms, networks, and advice businesses on the FCA's Advice Guidance Boundary Review (AGBR) and practical implementation of Targeted Support. He develops playbooks, script libraries, governance frameworks, and consumer journey designs. He is one of the few UK practitioners who bridges FCA policy design and real-world IFA delivery.

### targeted.support — Dedicated TS Platform

A standalone website and resource hub for the B2B Targeted Support work. Includes:
- Three white papers (2025–2026) covering market context, strategic implications, and behavioural execution
- Strategic services: Targeted Support Design Calls, Design Sprints, governance frameworks, implementation playbooks
- Positioning: "Board-level clarity" and "Governance-first" approach
- Central thesis: *"The advice gap is not just an advice problem. It is an execution problem."*

### Thought Leadership and Speaking

Patrick writes for IFA Magazine, delivers webinars and conference presentations, and contributes to industry debate on regulation, consumer outcomes, and technology. Featured on Bloomberg TV, CNBC, BBC, Radio 4, Simply Money, The Money Channel, and national press.

**Published White Papers (Financial Planning in Transition series):**

1. **"Aligning Client Value, Regulation, and Technology"** (November 2025) — The foundational paper. Synthesises research from Vanguard, Dimensional, Morningstar, EY, Accenture, and the Lang Cat. Argues the future is hybrid: human empathy + behavioural science + intelligent automation. Covers the advice gap (9% of UK adults pay for advice), Consumer Duty, and AGBR. Recommends Standardised TS Blueprints for smaller firms.

2. **"Beyond Targeted Support"** (December 2025) — Deep structural analysis of the TS regime and its competitive implications for UK financial services 2026–2030. Key arguments: TS favours data-rich scaled firms (platforms, workplace pensions, fintechs); the FCA is agnostic about delivery channel; consolidation will accelerate as an economic consequence; Emotional AI will reshape consumer engagement but remains years from regulated deployment; TS has a significant protection blind spot (only 29% of UK adults hold life insurance). Competitive map predicts market bifurcation into three tiers: mass market (automation), mass affluent (hybrid), HNW (bespoke).

3. **"From Advice to Execution: A Behavioural Framework for Sustained Client Outcomes"** (May 2026) — Introduces the **Behavioural Execution Architecture (BEA)**, Patrick's proprietary framework. Core argument: the primary failure in financial planning is not technical but behavioural — the intention-action gap. The BEA has six components (Clarity of Values and Goals, Decision Framing, Micro-actions, Automation, Accountability, Feedback Loops) across three layers (Clarity, Decision & Action, Execution). Grounded in Kahneman/Tversky, Vanguard's behavioural coaching research (150bps Advisor Alpha), and Consumer Duty alignment.

These papers establish Patrick as a credible, independent voice at the intersection of behavioural science, FCA regulation (AGBR/TS/Consumer Duty), and AI adoption in financial services.
